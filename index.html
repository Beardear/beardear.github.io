About Me

I am an algorithm engineer at ZEISS Microscopy, where I develop cutting-edge imaging algorithms and cloud infrastructure solutions for next-generation biomedical and semiconductor X-ray systems. My work spans computer vision, computational imaging, and scalable cloud computing‚Äîdriving innovative solutions that have generated millions in annual revenue. I hold an M.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis, where I specialized in deep learning for image reconstruction, classification and detection, developed and optimized rateless edge computing solutions. My research journey began at Southeast University, where I explored generative adversarial networks for image restoration and 3D computed tomography reconstruction. At Zeiss, I design and develop fully-3D diffusion-based vision foundation models for high-quality X-ray reconstruction and super-resolution. I created the patented "Throughput" mode technology that dramatically reduces sampling time, and engineered hybrid "2.5D" workflows for large field-of-view, high-resolution imaging. Beyond algorithms, I architected Azure cloud infrastructure that replaced expensive workstations with cost-effective subscription models. My research has been published in leading conferences and journals, including work on deep-learning-based resolution recovery and progressive neural compression. I'm passionate about pushing the boundaries of what's possible in computational imaging and making advanced technology more accessible through innovative engineering. Outside of research and engineering, I enjoy skiing, paddling, cycling and playing ping pong.

I have expertise in designing diffusion-based deep foundation models for X-ray reconstruction, leading Azure cloud-based development, and implementing LLM-empowered agents for various applications.

I hold a Master's degree in Computer Science from Washington University in St. Louis, and have published multiple papers in prestigious journals and conferences.
